Visitor Entry

Most Western nationals (US, UK, Canada, EU, Australia) can enter Saint Lucia as visitors without a prior visa for stays up to 6 weeks (42 days) initially, with extensions possible at the Immigration Department. The standard tourist entry allows up to 6 months total with extensions, provided you have:

  • A return or onward ticket
  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity beyond intended stay)
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Sufficient funds to support your stay (no fixed minimum, but be prepared to demonstrate)

Visitor status does not give you the right to work, operate a business, or access public services. For retirees planning a trial period before committing, visitor status for 3‚Äì6 months is a practical first step.

Temporary Residence Permit

Once you decide to make Saint Lucia your long-term base, you should apply for a Temporary Residence Permit (TRP). This is the standard entry point for non-CARICOM foreign nationals wishing to reside in Saint Lucia long-term without working in local employment.

  • Completed application form (from the Immigration Department, Castries)
  • Valid passport and copies of all pages
  • 2 recent passport-size photographs
  • Police certificate of good conduct from home country (less than 6 months old)
  • Medical certificate confirming good health from a licensed physician
  • Proof of financial sufficiency ‚Äî bank statements showing sufficient funds to support yourself without working (typically US$1,500‚Äì2,500/month minimum demonstrated income)
  • Proof of accommodation in Saint Lucia (lease agreement or property deed)
  • Character references (typically 2 letters from reputable individuals)
  • Processing fee: approximately EC$500‚Äì800 (US$185‚Äì296) for initial application
  • Permit fee: EC$500‚Äì1,000/year (US$185‚Äì370)

Processing time: typically 4‚Äì12 weeks. Apply well before your visitor status expires. Renewals are typically processed faster than initial applications.

TRPs are typically granted for 1 year and must be renewed annually. Renewal requires similar documentation to the initial application (updated police certificate, financial statements, medical certificate) and is generally straightforward if you have maintained good standing.

Important: Start your renewal process at least 2‚Äì3 months before the permit expiry. Immigration offices can be slow and appointment availability limited. Being in Saint Lucia on an expired permit, even briefly, can create complications for future renewals and permanent residence applications.

Some retirees who spend part of the year abroad (e.g., escaping hurricane season) find that maintaining a TRP requires demonstrating continued Saint Lucia ties ‚Äî retaining a rental property or home even while temporarily absent helps.

Permanent Residence

Permanent Residence in Saint Lucia gives you indefinite right to reside without annual renewals, and eventually opens the path to naturalisation (citizenship).

There is no fixed, publicly published minimum residency period for permanent residence. In practice, applicants typically need to have held a TRP and demonstrated genuine continuous residence for several years (commonly cited as 5+ years, though this is subject to discretion).

The application is considered on merit by the Governor General on the advice of the Chief Immigration Officer and Cabinet. Factors considered include:

  • Length of lawful residence in Saint Lucia
  • Financial self-sufficiency (demonstrated income from pension, investments, etc.)
  • Good character (clean criminal record throughout residence)
  • Investment in the country (property ownership, business activity)
  • Community integration
  • Health status (not a public charge risk)

Permanent residence is granted as a Permanent Residence Certificate. It does not automatically confer citizenship and does not give the right to vote.

After holding Permanent Residence, a foreign national may apply for Saint Lucian citizenship through naturalisation. This requires an additional period of permanent residence (the exact requirement is discretionary and set by Cabinet) with demonstrated good character and integration.

Naturalised citizenship grants full Saint Lucian citizenship including a Saint Lucian passport (visa-free access to 140+ countries, including the UK and Schengen Area). Saint Lucia permits dual citizenship, so you do not need to renounce your home country's citizenship in most cases ‚Äî check your home country's rules.

Citizenship by Investment (CBI)

Saint Lucia's CBI Programme, established by the Saint Lucia Citizenship by Investment Act (No. 14 of 2015) and administered by the Citizenship by Investment Unit (CIU), offers a fast-track route to full Saint Lucian citizenship for qualifying investors. This is entirely separate from the residence pathway and has no minimum stay requirement.

Why Retirees Consider CBI

The Saint Lucian passport provides visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 140+ countries including the entire UK, EU Schengen Zone, and many others. For retirees from countries with weaker passports, or those wanting a second citizenship for estate planning, travel flexibility, or future-proofing, CBI is a serious option. There is no requirement to actually live in Saint Lucia ‚Äî you can obtain citizenship and keep the passport without ever residing there.

The real estate investment is technically an asset purchase (not a donation), meaning you hold property with resale value after the 5-year lock-in. However, the investment must be in a CIU-approved project. Not all Saint Lucia property qualifies ‚Äî you must purchase specifically through approved developments listed by the CIU.

Additional costs: government processing fees (US$2,000‚Äì4,000), due diligence fees (US$7,500‚Äì15,000 per adult), attorney fees, and stamp duty on the real estate transaction.

Investment in a qualifying enterprise ‚Äî typically a major business project approved by the CIU. Minimum investment varies based on the nature and scale of the enterprise, typically US$3.5‚Äì6 million for a business employing Saint Lucian staff. This route is primarily relevant to business investors, not typical retirees.

Investment in approved Saint Lucia government bonds. Minimum investment US$500,000 (single), held for a minimum of 5 years. Interest rates are modest. This option is less popular than NEF or real estate due to higher minimum investment and lower perceived return, but the investment is recoverable after the holding period.

  1. Engage a CIU-Accredited Agent or Attorney All CBI applications must be submitted through an authorised agent ‚Äî you cannot apply directly. Your agent guides document collection, due diligence preparation, and submission. Choose carefully ‚Äî CBI agents vary significantly in quality.
  2. Document Preparation (4‚Äì8 weeks) Gather required documents: certified birth certificate, certified marriage certificate (if applicable), police clearances from all countries of residence in last 10 years, medical certificate, passport copies, bank statements demonstrating source of funds, professional references, and more.   3. Due Diligence (6‚Äì12 weeks) The CIU conducts thorough background checks on all adult applicants through an independent international due diligence firm. CARICOM Nationals

Citizens of CARICOM (Caribbean Community) member states ‚Äî including Jamaica, Barbados, Trinidad & Tobago, Guyana, Grenada, Dominica, Antigua, and others ‚Äî have significantly easier rights in Saint Lucia under the CARICOM Freedom of Movement agreements.

Skilled CARICOM nationals with the CARICOM Skills Certificate can live and work freely in Saint Lucia. For retirees from CARICOM countries, the path to long-term residence is substantially simpler and less expensive than for nationals from outside the Caribbean Community.

Alien Landholding Licence

Non-citizens wishing to purchase land or property in Saint Lucia must first obtain an Alien Landholding Licence under the Alien Landholding Regulation Act. This requirement applies to individuals who are not Saint Lucian citizens or CARICOM nationals exercising freedom of movement rights.

Apostille and Notarisation

Documents from most countries need to be apostilled (officially certified for international use under the Hague Convention) before submission to Saint Lucian authorities. This is done in your home country ‚Äî check your government's apostille service. Allow time: UK apostilles take 5‚Äì15 business days; US varies by state. Start gathering and apostilling documents before you arrive in Saint Lucia.

Finding a Reputable Immigration Lawyer

All immigration and CBI applications should be handled or reviewed by a licensed Saint Lucian attorney. The Saint Lucia Bar Association maintains a register of licensed attorneys. For CBI specifically, ensure your agent is on the CIU's official list of authorised agents (published on the CIU website: ciu.govt.lc).

Key questions to ask a potential immigration attorney:

  • Are you licensed by the Saint Lucia Bar Association? What is your licence number?
  • How many TRP / permanent residence / CBI applications have you handled in the past 3 years?
  • What is your total fee estimate for my situation (all-in, including disbursements)?
  • What is the realistic timeline for my application?
  • What are the most common reasons for rejection in cases like mine?
