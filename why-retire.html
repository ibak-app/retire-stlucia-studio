Saint Lucia covers 238 square miles and has a population of approximately 173,000 people. This intimacy is part of its charm â€” but it also means limited variety. There are a finite number of restaurants, a finite set of social circles, a finite number of activities. Many retirees find themselves making short regional trips regularly for variety, specialist shopping, or medical appointments.

Saint Lucia lies in the southern Lesser Antilles and statistically experiences fewer direct hurricane hits than islands further north. However, the risk is real. Hurricane Tomas (2010) caused significant damage. Tropical storms and the outer bands of distant hurricanes bring heavy rain, flooding, and landslides even when the eye passes north.

Countries like Panama (Pensionado visa), Costa Rica (Pensionado visa), and Portugal (D7 Passive Income visa) offer structured programmes specifically designed to attract foreign retirees, often including tax benefits, import duty exemptions on vehicles and household goods, and discounts on services.

CARICOM nationals (citizens of Caribbean Community member states) have easier freedom-of-movement rights and a simpler path to residency.
